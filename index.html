🧳 Tips & Packing

👗 Clothing

  • Lightweight sundresses (3–4)
  • Flowy maxi dresses for evenings + temples
  • Bikinis (3+) + rash guard for surf
  • Light cardigan for Ubud evenings
  • Sarong (buy in Bali, doubles as temple cover)
  • Comfortable walking sandals
  • Closed shoes for temple hikes

💄 Beauty & Health

  • SPF 50+ sunscreen (lots — expensive in Bali)
  • After-sun / aloe vera gel
  • DEET insect repellent (evenings)
  • Imodium + probiotics
  • Reef-safe sunscreen for Nusa Penida
  • Small first-aid kit
  • Prescription meds in carry-on

📱 Tech

  • Power bank (full-day outings!)
  • Waterproof phone case (waterfalls)
  • Universal adapter (Indonesia: Type C/F)
  • Telkomsel SIM (best coverage)
  • GoPro for surf + snorkel
  • Download Grab, Gojek, Maps offline

💳 Money & Safety

  • A$1 ≈ IDR 10,700
  • Use bank ATMs only (not street ATMs)
  • Tell your bank before travel
  • Keep bags front-facing in crowds
  • Emergency: 112 (universal), 119 (ambulance)

🙏 Cultural Etiquette

  • Sarong + covered shoulders at temples
  • Remove shoes before entering homes/temples
  • Don't step on offerings on the ground
  • Use right hand to give/receive
  • Ask before photographing locals
  • Quiet voices in temple compounds

🍽 Food Tips

  • Bottled or filtered water only
  • Must-try: nasi goreng, sate lilit, gado gado, tempeh manis, es cendol
  • Ubud = Bali's best plant-based food
  • Jimbaran seafood beach dinner = best value
  • Bintang beer: local, cold, cheap

📸 Instagram Tips

  • Swing: arrive 8:30am for best light
  • Tegallalang: overcast light > harsh sun
  • Tanah Lot: 30 min before sunset
  • Kelingking: arrive FIRST before tour groups
  • Wear bright/bold colours in jungle
  • Flowy dresses move beautifully on swings

🚗 Getting Around

  • Grab: reliable, metered (like Uber)
  • Gojek: add motorbike option for 1 person
  • Private driver ~A$50–80/day (split 3 ways)
  • Do NOT rent scooter unless experienced
  • Airport: Grab pick-up inside terminal
